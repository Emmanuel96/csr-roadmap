CSR Road Map - Where are you?

This exercise is designed to give participants 'snapshot' of where their organization is located on the CSR road map of zero to full engagement. The questoins included are generic with some flexibility for individual or specific action to influence the results. For a full list of CSR activity categorised under the CSR-A Four Pillars.

How to use the CSR road map...

This road map is intended to give you a quick visual representation of where your organisation sits in regard to socially responsible activity currently undertaken.

The road map is separated into 4 sections that correspond to our Four Pillars system - Environment, Workplace, Community and Philanthropy. Each Pillar has 5 questions, with 4 possible responses and an optional 6th input that allows you to score for activity outside the scope of the questions.

Simply respond to the questions by checking the boxes that describes your level of activity. Because the roap map works by counting your positive responses you must click the boxes to the left of your answer too, so if you are involved in leading action also check the limited and positive boxes to score 3.

When you have answered the questions count up all the positive 'green' squares and enter them into the total bar at the bottom of the page and more on to the next Pillar.

When you have completed all Four Pillars go to the results page which reveals you where you are on your journey...

    Benefits of good CSR policy

    Achieving CSR Accreditation offers your organisation the opportunity to talk about the positive activity you are engaged in. It is an invaluable marketing tool for spreading the word about what a great organisation you are, both to work with and to work for. Accreditation is a powerful message to promote companies on social media channels. People want to read stories with positive purpose and outcomes.

    These stories are essential to building your brand reputation, overall profitablity and bottom line.

    CSR Impact Reporting

    Social and environmental purpose with profit.

    A CSR Impact Report is similar to an Annual Report and shows all your audiences the impact that your organisation is making through its CSR commitments. This includes financial benefits, environmental impact, staff and stakeholder engagement, positive impact on communities and charities.

    Submitted as part of a CSR Accreditation application or based on your successful CSR Accreditation, the report becomes an essential tool for auditing, benchmarking, measuring and reporting your CSR impact. This will include advisories for future development.
